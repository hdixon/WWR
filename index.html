Wireless World Radio is bringing you stories of all flavors – from the ordinary yet hectic narrative that happens behind the scenes planning a big party to thinking about deeper questions on the edge of human knowledge.

Wireless World was formed by a small group of Carnegie Mellon University students to tell great stories. Walking around a college campus like CMU's, you notice glimpses of cool things happening all around you. One thing that makes CMU unique is that everyone is working to build something: professors are developing self-driving cars with GM and Uber, artists are using technology to make robotic structures capable of holding the weight of a person, and researchers are making discoveries in every corner of academia which push our world forward.

But Wireless World is not merely about notifying the world that someone at CMU did something cool. Wireless World is about stories. It's about weaving a narrative formed by science, technology, art, people, and relationships that say something about what it means to be human.
